# Zombies!!! Official Rules Reference

Summaries and official Q&A from Twilight Creations, Inc. rulesets in `.claude/rules/`.
Clarifications sourced from `zombies rules clairification.txt` (official rulings by Kerry Breitenstein).

## Zombies!!! (Base Game)
**File:** `Zombies!!!.pdf`

The original game. Players start at Town Square, draw and place tiles to build the city, fight zombies, and race to reach the center of the Helipad (killing the zombie on it) or collect 25 kills to win.

**Core Rules:**
- Movement: roll d6, move up to that many spaces orthogonally (no diagonal movement)
- Combat: roll d6, need 4+ to kill a zombie. On fail: spend bullets (+1 each) or hearts (reroll)
- Losing all hearts: respawn at Town Square, lose half kills (rounded down), reset to 3 hearts/3 bullets
- Event cards: play 1 per round (start of your turn to start of your next turn). Can be played anytime including mid-combat
- Items/weapons: can have as many as you want but only one copy of each kind at a time
- Can only use ONE weapon per combat (exception: "Hey Look A Shotgun" can combine with others)
- Hearts capped at 5; bullets are unlimited
- You cannot kill other players
- Players and zombies cannot move diagonally
- Tiles that cannot be legally placed are removed from play; player does not place a tile that turn
- Win condition: reach the CENTER SQUARE of the Helipad and kill the zombie on it (unless Alternate Food Source is in play)
- Lines in streets mean nothing (decorative only)

**Named Buildings:**
- Players may enter named buildings from any adjacent street space (doors are not required — clarified in Z6/ZX)
- Cannot move on grass or parking lot squares (exception: Z5 parking lots are accessible)

**Official Card Rulings (Base Game):**

*General:*
- Cards can be played anytime including mid-combat or mid-movement; only 1 per round
- Event deck IS reshuffled when exhausted
- You cannot play cards targeting an "opponent" on yourself
- A card placed in front of you is "in play" and does NOT count against your hand limit
- A card already placed in front of you can be discarded/activated anytime — this is NOT "playing a card" (it was already played); does not count against your 1-card-per-round limit
- All item cards are weapons — even the skateboard
- You may have as many weapons as you want but only one of each kind at a time
- When you die, the rest of your turn continues normally (roll zombies, discard) — only your movement ends; you can still play cards

*Specific Cards:*
- **Alternate Food Source**: stops ALL zombies everywhere from attacking all players. You MAY NOT kill zombies while it is in effect. You CAN still use a Grenade (interrupts their dinner). If a zombie is on the Helipad center square, you can still win (you just don't have to kill it)
- **I Don't Think They're Dead**: opponent rolls the die TWICE. If either roll is 1, 2, or 3, two zombies must be returned to the pool. Bullets and hearts can be used to modify as normal. If they only have 1 zombie, they still roll twice — but can't discard 2 zombies, so they just have to succeed on both rolls
- **Claustrophobia**: target must exit the building by the shortest path. They may continue movement after leaving the building but cannot re-enter until their next turn
- **Much Needed Rest**: CAN be played during "Fear." CANNOT be played during "Hysterical Paralysis"
- **Hysterical Paralysis**: target loses their entire turn — including fighting a zombie already on their space. No turn = no fight
- **Fear**: played mid-turn → takes effect on the target's NEXT turn (does not affect current turn movement)
- **Bad Sense of Direction**: player is returned to Town Square but may continue using remaining movement after being placed there. If played on a Fear'd player, it can only take effect after their turn (not during)
- **Where Did Everybody Go**: player still fights any zombies they move through
- **The Keys Are Still In It**: still fight zombies normally when sharing a square. "Your Shoe's Untied" cannot reduce this movement (no movement roll was made). Skateboard does not add +2 (no movement roll). Fear still prevents movement even with this card (still considered movement)
- **Grenade / Molotov Cocktail**: range limited to the building you are currently in OR the space directly in front of the door. Molotov lasts one turn only
- **Butterfingers**: the player who PLAYED the card decides which weapon/item is discarded. Can only target weapons/items in play (face-up on table) — cannot target cards in an opponent's hand
- **Look A Shotgun / Hey Look A Shotgun**: place in front of you; discarded after 3 combats (place 3 bullets on the card, remove one per combat). Fire Axe still gives its bonus when combined with this card
- **Adrenaline Rush** (doubles movement): add Skateboard bonus BEFORE doubling (Skateboard adds first, then double the total)
- **Brain Cramp**: since you take their movement for them, you can decide the target moves 0 spaces
- **You Lookin At Me**: cancels the targeted card. If the card targeted cannot hit another player (e.g. "Adjusting Nicely"), it is just discarded with no effect
- **Lots of Luck with That**: must be played while YOU are moving — cannot play it when another player moves into your square. Can play during your turn or if someone uses a card to move you

## Zombies!!! 2: Zombie Corps(e)
**File:** `Z2.pdf`

Army base expansion. Introduces **government-enhanced zombies** and the Jeep vehicle.

**Key Rules:**
- **Government-Enhanced Zombies** (glowing zombies from the Top Secret Lab tile):
  - Require a roll of **5 or 6** to kill (not 4+)
  - Move **2 spaces** per zombie movement phase (not 1)
  - May not move through a space containing another zombie
- **Jeep** (Motor Pool tile): doubles movement roll as long as player stays on road spaces. Lost on death or entering a building. Can be used to kill zombies (target still 4/5/6). Motor Pool counts as an interior space (not eligible for mine field card effects)
- City streets and army base streets may NOT connect (except through The Front Gate)
- Bullets and hearts may only modify combat rolls unless a card says otherwise
- "Alternate Food Source" — you MAY NOT kill zombies while it is in play
- Cannot place or move zombies into unnamed buildings
- Cannot kill other players

**If Neither Helipad Can Be Placed:**
- Both are discarded; only win condition is 25 kills
- If Helipads were shuffled into tile decks and tiles remain, they can be reshuffled back in

**Official Card Rulings (Z2):**
- **What Is That Smell**: target MUST leave the tile immediately — cannot choose to stay
- **Weekend Pass Denied**: the only cards that can be played before discarding hand are ones that would affect Weekend Pass Denied itself
- **Rocket Launcher**: CAN destroy a tile leaving other players stranded (they must reconnect or die to return to Town Square). CANNOT destroy Town Square
- **Dynamite**: diagonal spaces are NOT considered adjacent (to avoid confusion since diagonal movement is not allowed)
- **Butterfingers on a just-played Dynamite**: yes — Butterfingers can target it and the Dynamite does not go off
- **Breakthrough**: paths created can be used by zombies
- **You Lookin At Me** with no legal target: the targeted card is just discarded (e.g. "We're Screwed")

**Official Tile Rulings (Z2):**
- **Jeep**: lost on death — does not respawn with you
- **Helipad spaces**: considered road spaces — you can drive the Jeep all the way to the helicopter
- **Killing with the Jeep**: target number is still 4, 5, or 6

## Zombies!!! 3: Mall Walkers
**File:** `Zombies 3 Rules.pdf`

Mall expansion. Introduces air ducts for inter-store movement.

**Key Rules:**
- Mall tiles must connect via hallways (hallways must align even if using ducts)
- **Air Ducts**: players can move from one store to an adjacent store (including diagonal adjacency) via ducts without using hallways. Must END movement on a duct space; on next turn, skip movement roll and teleport to other store's duct space. Zombies cannot use ducts
- **Escalator tile** (*): everything connected to it is on the 2nd floor. 1st floor stores may NOT connect to 2nd floor stores. Helipad MUST attach to 2nd floor and appears after the Escalator
- Cards with "town," "road," or "building" only apply to town tiles, not mall tiles; cards with "hall" or "store" cannot be used in town/road/building
- Any card placed in front of a player counts as a weapon/item for Butterfingers purposes

**Official Rulings (Z3):**
- **Mixing Z3 with base/Z2**: yes. Do not need Z2 to play Z3 — but you'll need its zombies/tokens
- **Duct adjacency**: stores are adjacent if ANY part of them is touching, including diagonal
- **Zombie moved to your square while you declared duct use**: you still fight it
- **Sprinkler card**: only affects zombies inside the mall, not outside
- **Helipad can't be placed in mall**: if no tiles remain, discard it. If tiles remain, shuffle it back in
- **Helipad drawn before Escalator**: shuffle it back into the tile stack; that player draws another tile

**Bonus Scenarios included:** Search and Rescue, Kill the Guy With the Keys

## Zombies!!! 4: The End...
**File:** `Z4 Rules Final copy.pdf`

Woods/cabin expansion. Introduces **zombie dogs** and the Book of the Dead pages.

**Key Rules:**
- **Zombie Dogs**: move 1 or 2 spaces per zombie movement (do NOT have to move 2). Max 2 dogs per square (including when passing through). Kill threshold: **5 or 6**. Deal only 1/2 heart damage. Multiple dogs on same square when tile is placed is allowed
- No roads on tiles (except the Bridge) — tiles placed in any orientation as long as adjacent. Do not deliberately block a door or entrance
- Start on the Bridge tile; first tile connects off the bridge end only
- Players start with 3 bullets, 2 hearts, 2 half-hearts, 3 event cards
- Win condition: reach the Cabin, clear all zombies from it, and perform the spell — OR kill 25 zombies
- **Pages from the Book of the Dead**: are items. Must be placed in front of you first (not played directly from hand). Discard from play to activate. Max 1 placed per turn, max 1 discarded per turn
- Turn order: Draw tile → Place tile → Combat current space → Draw events → Roll move → Move → Roll zombies → Optional discard
- Buildings and locations are NOT the same thing — cards that say "building" do not apply to locations like the Altar or Campground
- Can have more than one item in play at a time (but never two of the same item)
- Can use more than one item during combat

**Official Card Rulings (Z4):**
- **Two zombie dogs on same square**: completely separate combat rolls for each
- **Pet Cemetery + "Here Doggie!"**: max 18 dogs on the cemetery. When the card comes out, fill up to 9 dogs — can double up (2 per square)
- **Clair Warlock**: opponent moved to tile adjacent to THEIR current location (not yours)
- **Something Doesn't Feel Quite Right**: can only remove non-Book of the Dead Pages from the draw deck or discard pile

**Official Token Rulings (Z4):**
- **Tokens on tiles without buildings**: place on any square, max one token per square
- **Boathouse**: only 2 building squares — third token goes on the dock (considered part of the boathouse)
- **Hearts cap**: if you have 4.5 hearts and land on a heart token, you CANNOT pick it up. You may never exceed 5 hearts and may never discard a heart to pick up another

## Zombies!!! 5: School's Out Forever
**File:** `Z5 Rules Final copy.pdf`

College campus expansion. Introduces **guts tokens** and a dynamic hand limit.

**Key Rules:**
- **Guts Tokens**: start with 3. Roll unmodified 6 in combat → gain 1 guts. Roll 1 in combat → lose 1 guts. Max 5 guts
- Hand limit = number of guts tokens (minimum 1 card always kept)
- If guts < cards at end of turn: discard down to match guts count
- Death resets to 3 life, 3 bullets, 3 guts
- School entrance tile triggers integration with base set; Z5 tiles must attach to the school
- Z5 Helipad attaches to any building (must go through the building to reach it)
- Parking lot tiles are fully accessible by players and zombies (overrides base game ruling)

## Zombies!!! 6: Six Feet Under
**File:** `Zombies 6 Rules.pdf`

Fully mixed expansion (shuffle tiles and cards directly into base set). Introduces sewers and subway.

**Key Rules:**
- **Subway Stations**: named buildings (*). Enter and declare subway use → skip next turn entirely (except fighting zombies on your current square) → on the following turn, teleport to any other subway station on the board. If no other stations are on the board, ability cannot be used
- **Sewer Tokens**: 2 per player. Place on street spaces only (cannot move once placed). Enter sewer by declaring intent + laying pawn on its back. Each turn in sewer: pay 1 heart (or guts) → move normally but skip all zombie combat. Zombies cannot enter sewers. Exit at any sewer token space. You CAN die in the sewer
- Rules clarification: you may enter named buildings from ANY adjacent street space — doors not required

## Zombies!!! 8: Jailbreak
**File:** `Z8 Rules Final copy.pdf`

Prison expansion. Introduces **dodge** mechanic and straightjackets.

**Key Rules:**
- All players start in the Mental Ward in a straightjacket
- **Straightjacket**: at start of your turn, roll d6 — on 6, you're free (no more rolling). While in jacket: roll 3+ to dodge (skip combat without dying, don't collect zombie). Normal kill (4+) still works. If you die in the jail, you must roll again on your next turn
- **Dodge rule** (optional, usable with any set): roll 3 = dodge and continue moving without collecting zombie. Roll 4+ = kill as normal. When landing on a zombie with no remaining movement, roll anyway — if you fail the dodge, roll again at the start of your next turn
- When playing with base set: remove Town Square, add jail entrance; Helipad shuffled into middle of stack. Tiles from jail not drawn until at least one player has escaped

## Zombies!!! 9: Ashes to Ashes *(Scavenged)*
**File:** `Zombies!!! 9 Rules FINAL copy.pdf`

Graveyard expansion. Introduces "Automatic" event cards.

**Key Rules:**
- **Automatic Cards**: say "Automatic" on the card. Trigger immediately when drawn. Must resolve before continuing. Count as one of your drawn cards (you don't draw replacements immediately)
- Rules clarification: you may enter named buildings from any adjacent street space (doors not required)
- For quicker games: preset base tiles, place expansion entrances, players start at Town Square
- After Helipad revealed, optionally roll 2d6 for movement

## Zombies!!! X: Feeding the Addiction *(10th Anniversary)*
**File:** `Z!!!X Rules Final copy.pdf`

10th anniversary expansion. Introduces **Addiction Cards**.

**Key Rules:**
- **Addiction Cards** (optional, blue-bordered): deal one face-up to each player at game start. Represent personal handicaps visible to all. Each adds extra steps to that player's turn. Addiction cards override rules on contradiction
- **Automatic Cards** (same as Z9): trigger on draw, resolve immediately. If more than one is drawn, each resolves in order before the next
- Rules clarification: you may enter named buildings from any adjacent street space
- Tiles and regular event cards mixed with base set. Addiction cards kept separate

## Zombies!!! 11: Death Inc.
**File:** `Z!!!11 Rules Final copy.pdf`

Office building expansion. Unique board setup and win condition.

**Key Rules:**
- **Pre-set board**: hallway tiles form a connected loop (4 tiles per side, T-tiles connecting). Entrance tile on one end. Players start at center of Entrance
- **Movement boost**: movement roll = d6 + current number of life tokens
- **Win condition**: save the Board of Directors. Rescue surviving board members and hold a final board meeting — NOT escape to Helipad or 25 kills
- **Zombie Office Workers (ZOW)**: no special rules, treated as standard zombies
- **Pulling Strings cards**: separate deck from event cards. Kept separate during setup
- Director tokens and ZCEO token used for win condition mechanics
- Turn order: Combat current space → Draw tile → Place tile → Draw events → Roll move + life → Move → Combat during move → Roll zombies → Optional discard

## Zombies!!! 12: Zombie Zoo
**File:** `Z12 Rules FINAL copy.pdf`

Zoo expansion. Introduces animal zombie types and Lock Tokens.

**Key Rules:**
- **Pre-set board**: all tiles placed face-up at start (no drawing during play)
- **Animal Zombies**: Lions, Tigers, Monkeys, Hyenas, Gorillas, Polar Bears — each in their own cage tile
- **Lock Tokens**: placed on cage access points ("60:00" side up). Players land on them to override the automatic release. After collecting, player moves to adjacent tile; the cage tile is REMOVED from play
- **Automatic Release Mechanism**: in 2-4 players, when any player reaches 6 kills → flip/remove locks. In 5-6 players, trigger at 4 kills
- **Player death**: eliminated but can still play cards (no more draws). Accomplishments still count
- **Win condition**: player with the most points wins. 1 pt/zombie kill, 5 pts/lock token, 5 pts for last survivor
- Event deck NOT reshuffled when exhausted
- Automatic cards: only one activates per turn even if you have multiple in hand

## Zombies!!! 13: DEFCON Z
**File:** `Zombies 13 rules copy.pdf`

City compound expansion. Introduces **infection counters** and special named location bonuses.

**Key Rules:**
- **Infection**: roll each turn if you have infection counters. If result < counter count → gain 1 more. At 6 counters → become a zombie (removed from board, replaced with zombie figure; lose half kills + all weapons)
- **Rolling natural 1 in combat** = bitten → gain 1 infection counter (even if you still kill the zombie)
- Players start with 5 hearts and 5 bullets
- **Win condition (competitive)**: first to reach Helipad with 15+ kills AND 3 or fewer infection counters
- **Win condition (cooperative)**: all players reach Helipad with 10+ kills each, no player died of infection
- **Special named tiles** (clear of zombies to activate benefit):
  - Foodlandia: gain 1 heart
  - Nether Worlder Armory: gain 1 bullet; or spend 2 turns to draw any weapon card
  - US Border Patrol: discard any cards and draw the same number
  - Gallow Mountain Tower: draw top 3 tiles and reorder them
  - McCormick Animal Hospital: lose 1 infection counter
- **Dr. Tilton pawn**: special ability to attempt to "turn" a zombie (roll 2d6, need 10+). Can control up to 3 zombies
- When Helipad drawn: player with FEWEST kills places it (ties broken by most infection counters, then die roll)

## Zombies!!! 14: Space Bites
**File:** `Zombies 14 Rules copy.pdf`

Space station expansion. Pre-set board, shuttle mechanic, ranged combat.

**Key Rules:**
- **Pre-set board**: Docking Bays on sides, corners at corners, Named Tiles fill the rest
- **Movement**: players and zombies may only move tile-to-tile through air lock spaces
- **Gravity and Life Support** (die on each Named Tile, starts at 6): when combat fails before bullets, lower die by 1. At 3: -1 penalty to combat and movement. At 1: tile flips — inaccessible for rest of game
- **Ranged Combat**: kill zombies 1-2 squares away with a ranged weapon. 1 square = need 5+. 2 squares = need 6+. Can spend bullets. Hearts optional (reroll only). Can end ranged combat anytime (zombie can't hurt you at range)
- **Shuttle**: starts with die showing 2 next to Docking Bay. End of Shuttle Tracker's turn: 2→1→arrives. Docks for 2 turns, absent for 2 turns. Randomly determines which Docking Bay. Win by reaching center square of Shuttle's Docking Bay tile
- **Zombie Placement**: after zombie movement each turn, refill Named Tiles to 3 zombies on path squares if below 3
- Event deck NOT reshuffled (competitive). IS reshuffled (cooperative)
- **Cooperative**: all players must board same Shuttle. Draw 1 card, must play one of the 2 cards in hand each turn. Zombies always move toward players

## Zombies!!! 15: Another One Bites The Dust
**File:** `Zombies 15 Rules.pdf`

Desert/Las Vegas expansion. Introduces water tokens and visibility rules.

**Key Rules:**
- Players start with 3 hearts, 3 bullets, **2 water tokens**
- Starting on each player's second turn: discard 1 water token per turn. No water = lose 1 heart instead. Max 5 water tokens
- **Visibility** (rolled by starting player each round): roll 1 or 2 = visibility reduced by 1 → all players move 1 less space that round AND can only target players on same or adjacent tile with cards
- **Oasis Tiles**: place water tokens equal to player count when placed. Each round, add 1 water token to each Oasis that has been visited
- **Win condition**: first to reach the center square of the Entrance tile (Las Vegas)
- **Player vs Player Combat** (competitive only): both players on same square roll d6. Bullets usable. Higher roll wins, takes up to 2 water tokens from loser. Each player can only be targeted once per game
- Event deck NOT reshuffled when exhausted
- **Cooperative**: all players must reach Entrance. If anyone dies, all lose. Players may share water tokens on same square
